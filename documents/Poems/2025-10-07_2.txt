I see a sapling in the LORD's domain,
Bent low by tribulation, need, and pain.

Troubled, perplexed, pursued, and cast down,
Flooded with waters, yet impossible to drown.

It is a path to ruin, judged by the eye,
Believe not this, a careless, hollow lie.

By all appearance, this young tree will die,
But this is how the Husbandman makes fruit multiply.

Though storms of poisoned doctrine rage and pound,
It longs for the riches that cannot be found.

His everlasting root provides all that he needs,
He patiently endures to bear his cherished seed.

Precious fruit-bearing seed, the tree doubtless rejoices,
And sings its glad tidings with happy voices.

The incorruptible seed, for every generation,
Goes from one tree to another, to every people and nation.

To what shall we liken the kingdom of God?
A garden where wheat and tares share the sod.

It all began as one grain of mustard seed,
A mighty tree it became, the first and last of its breed.

Enduring all to bring forth fruit with glee,
The world ever wonders, "How can this be?"

The root cries in the streets: here's what you cannot see!
Your root is rotten, be grafted onto me.

A wild olive tree cannot abide in that day,
Nor will anything built with stubble, wood, or hay.

Be ready for that day, your lamps trimmed and bright,
Forsake the tempting urge to wander from the light.

Be ready for a day to walk straight into death, without a fear,
As wheat that spends its final breath to make the fruit appear.

How precious indeed the gifts that only God can give,
For without something worth dying for, can a man truly live?
