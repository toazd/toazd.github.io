A young tree in the garden of the LORD you see,
Struggling with tribulation, hunger, and poverty.

Troubled, perplexed, persecuted, and cast down,
Flooded with waters, yet impossible to drown.

A recipe for disaster if we judge with our eyes,
Let us not be deceived with such careless lies.

By all appearance this tree will soon be deceased,
But the husbandman prescribed exactly this for increase.

Poisoned doctrine tossed all about as the fury of the storm pounds,
The tree desireth none other than the true riches that cannot be found.

His everlasting root provides all that he needs,
He patiently endures to bare cherished seed.

Precious fruit born as seed the tree now doubtless rejoicing,
Merrily dispersing it to other fields, it's glad tidings voicing.

Incorruptable seed that endures from generation to generation,
From one tree to another and from one people to all nations.

To what shall we liken the kingdom of God?
An eclectic garden where both wheat and tares share the sod.

It all started with a tiny grain of mustard seed,
Into a mighty tree it has grown, the first and last of its' breed.

Enduring all things, bringing forth everlasting fruit with glee,
The world around the tree wonders ever, how can this be?

The root cries in the streets: come I will show you what you cannot see!
It's your hidden root that is rotten, ye must be graffed into me.

A wild olive tree can not survive in that day,
Nor will anything built with stubble, wood, or hay.

Be ready for that day, with your lamps trimmed and bright,
Forsaking every temptation to venture out of the light.

Be ready for a day to walk straight into death, without a fear,
As wheat that spends its final breath to make the fruit appear.

How precious indeed the gifts that only God can give,
For without something worth dying for, can a man truly live?
